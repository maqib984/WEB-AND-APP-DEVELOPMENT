KARAHI MENU

Karahi is a popular and aromatic dish originating from the Indian subcontinent, particularly known in Pakistani and North Indian cuisine. It is a savory delight prepared by cooking tender pieces of meat, such as chicken, mutton, or beef, in a thick and rich tomato-based gravy. The dish gets its name from the traditional round-bottomed, cast-iron cooking pot known as the "karahi." The meat is typically marinated with a blend of spices, including coriander, cumin, turmeric, and chili powder, giving it a flavorful and slightly spicy taste. The karahi is often garnished with fresh coriander leaves and served alongside naan or rice,making it a mouthwatering choice for enthusiasts of South Asian cuisine.
