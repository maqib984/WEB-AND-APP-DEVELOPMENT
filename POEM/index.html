If----

--- by Rudyard Kipling ---

if you can keep your head when all about you
Are losing theirs and blaming it on you,
If you can trust yourself when all men doubt you,
But make allowance for their doubting too,
If you can wait and note be tired by waiting,
Or being lied about, don't give away to hating.
And yet don't look too good, nor talk too wise;

If you can dream---and not make dreams your master;
If you think---and not thoughts your aim;
If you can meet with Triumph and Diasser
And treat those two impostors just the same;
If you can bear to bear the truth you've spoken
Twisted by kanves to make a trap for foods,
Or watch the things you gave your life to broken.
And stoop and build'en up with worm-out tools:

if you can make one reap of all your winning;
